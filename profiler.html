RADAR WIND PROFILERS


DeTect is the global leader in Radar Wind Profiler technologies with a full range of modern, state-of-the-art wind profiler products that includes the world's only network class Doppler radar wind profiler system.

Advanced Radar Wind Profilers:

The RAPTOR wind profiler technology from DeTect incorporates advanced software, high-speed processors, and more capable and as faster analog-to-digital converters (ADC's) with on-board full digital-down-converters. The RAPTOR new generation radar wind profilers are more reliable, more capable, and produce higher quality wind speed and profile measurement than conventional systems. RAPTOR uses the latest in digital processing technology and functionality delivering significantly improved system performance and reliability over legacy designs.

DeTect’s Meteorological Systems Group in Longmont, Colorado USA provides a broad range of experience in design, development, testing, installation, operation, maintenance, data analysis and support on operational and research wind profiler systems worldwide. This experience includes development and application of advanced signal processing techniques for wind profilers for government, aviation, aerospace and industrial clients. Relevant experience also includes manufacture and installation of traditional coaxial-collinear (COCO) antennas for 50, 404, 449, and 482 MHz radar wind profilers and modernization of these older systems. DeTect also manufactures and supports the RAPTOR line that includes mesospheric, tropospheric and stratospheric systems and smaller boundary layer systems in a wide range of frequencies that includes trailered, mobile systems. RAPTOR technology can also be added as an upgrade to most OEM systems, including the LAP®-3000.

RAPTOR Radar Wind Profiler systems include the following models, frequency and power configurations:

  • FBS-MST 30-50 MHz Mesosphere, Stratosphere, Troposphere
  • FBS-STN 400-500 MHz Stratosphere Troposphere (Network Class)
  • FBS-TR 400-500 MHz Troposphere
  • XBS-TR 400-500 MHz Troposphere (fixed or mobile configurations)
  • VAD-BL 900-1300 MHz Parabolic Dish Boundary Layer (fixed high performance)
  • XBS-BL 900-1300 MHz Boundary Layer (fixed and mobile systems)
  • FMC-BL 900-1300 MHz Shipborne Boundary Layer (stabilized antenna system)
  • DBS-BL LAP-3000 Boundary Layer Upgrade

Scalable & Expandable Technology:

RAPTOR represents the state-of-the-art in profiler technology and incorporates modern software, advanced signal processing techniques and data acquisition hardware. RAPTOR models have been developed from the “ground up” with renewed emphasis on reliability and long-term sustainability. The RAPTOR design meets a wide spectrum of user needs from large Mesospheric-Stratospheric-Tropospheric profilers to small, portable and shipborne planetary boundary-layer (BL) systems. For most Stratospheric and Tropospheric (ST) applications, RAPTOR systems are built to operate in the 400–500 MHz band. RAPTOR ST systems employ a unique thinned Yagi antenna array, which along with the transmitter, is designed to be scalable so that custom configurations can easily be derived from a single design to meet target wind height availability requirements for each user.

Systems are modular and scalable so that the antenna size and power can be increased and expanded as user requirements evolve or as additional funding becomes available. For Boundary Layer only applications, systems in the 800-1500 MHz range are available. The RAPTOR BL systems use micro-patch or DeTect's proprietary VAD dish antenna technologies for compact systems that can be delivered in fixed or mobile (trailered) configurations. The solid-state phase-shifters in RAPTOR have no moving parts, no expensive consumables and are extremely reliable. DeTect's steerable beam technology also enables the radar to form a beam at any azimuth angle (0 to 360 degrees) and at any zenith angle from 0 to 21 degrees.

Software Application Suite:

The RAPTOR system is controlled by a graphics-rich suite of user-friendly software that includes DeTect's ASPEN data display system and the BIRCH Health and Status Monitor (HSM). A comprehensive set of data displays are available for all aspects of profiler operations and for standard and custom data products. The system also includes a real-time health and status monitoring and reporting systems with automatic alerting of exception events. The system is fully remote controllable with data displays available to multiple remote users in real-time. RAPTOR can be supplied with Microsoft Windows or Linux as the base operating systems. Highly advanced signal processing is used to determine and isolate meteorological signal in backscatter data, producing high quality data products. All system features and functions are accessible by users in real-time and during system operations.

Representative Projects

US National Weather Service Next Generation NOAA Profiler Network (NPN), USA - Network class 449 MHz RAPTOR systems used for severe storm forecasting and weather alerts. The NPN consists of 33 profilers located the central US in 'Tornado Alley' (www.profiler.noaa.gov/npn).

Department of Meteorology, City of Mexico City, Mexico - Boundary Layer Velocity Azimuth Display 915 MHz RAPTOR VAD-BL (patented) parabolic dish radar wind profiler provides wind data for the city's air quality monitoring and volcanic ash detection program. DeTect's contract included supply of a Radiometrics MP-3000A Temperature Thermodynamic and Liquid Profiler.

National Oceanographic and Atmospheric Administration, Washington, DC - Ship-mounted RAPTOR Radar Wind Profiler with automated beam steering for operation on ocean going vessel. US Department of Energy ordered the system for installation on a NOAA research vessel. It is used to develop high level wind profiles over the world's oceans.

Samoa Met Service, Samoa - RAPTOR VAD-BL 915 MHz Radar Wind Profiler and Radio Acoustic Sounding System (RASS) for weather and severe storm forecasting on the island nation of Samoa in the South Pacific. DeTect's scope of work included supply of the system, installation and start-up support and output of real-time BUFR compatible data for World Meteorological Organization (WMO) compliance.

Maryland Department of the Environment (MDOE) Wind Profiler Network, Maryland USA - DeTect provided RAPTOR upgrades for two existing Vaisala LAP®-3000 915 MHz radar wind profilers for improved system performance and reliability, and supplied a new RAPTOR VAD-BL profiler. MDOE uses the systems to correlate winds with air pollution data as part of the state's Clean Air Act compliance and monitoring program (MDE website).

Indian Air Force Aerostat Support Profilers, India - Two 449 MHz RAPTOR Radar Wind Profilers supporting IAF surveillance aerostats for border security. The systems are located at Barnala and Mothala, India, and DeTect provides on-going operations, maintenance and warranty support for the systems.

University of Alabama Huntsville Meteorological Research Department, Huntsville, Alabama, USA - RAPTOR XPR digital X-band fixed-beam vertical profile (FBVPR) precipitation radar for the Mobile Integrated Profiling System (MIPS). The UAH Severe Weather Group uses this system to support weather research programs at the University (UAH website).

US Army Dugway Proving Ground, Utah USA - 449 MHz RAPTOR Radar Wind Profiler used for modeling of chemical and biological agent dispersion for emergency response and national defense. The profiler is one of the remote sensing systems at the DPG Major Range and Test Facility Base (MRTFB).
